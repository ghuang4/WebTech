Brooklyn Caribbean Cuisine and Community

One of the key components to the creation of Caribbean food was during the time of colonialization when Africans were taken from their lands and settled in completely new areas, bringing with them their way of life. Not only were new foods introduced but new cooking styles were introduced and many dishes were born. Within the Caribbean culture, food is an essential component to many family traditions and is comprised of all the favorite seafood, chicken, and steak prepared with spices and colorful ingredients of the islands. The Caribbean cuisine also includes influences from other areas such as Africa, Spain, India, Dutch, American, Britain and Asia. ONe of the staple dishes in Caribbean cuisine is rice as is in Asian cuisine as well and each island has a specific type of rice dish. Some rice is seasons or served with coconut while others are yellow and brown rice served as a side along with other infamous dishes. In addition to the rice, beans are another key player in the mix and one that is served almost all the time. Amongst all the five boroughs, Brooklyn definitely inhabits one of the largest Caribbean communities in all of New York. In the 1940s, there was a huge influx of Caribbean immigrants coming over because many Caribbean islands and regions threw off their colonial governments and became indepedent nations. These communities created and will continue to maintain the soul of their traditions as they continue to share their delicious cooking and create communal spaces for people to come together and enjoy. Caribbean food today has truly evolved and there are so many dishes to discover and so, here are some of the best Caribbean spots to tryout while in Brooklyn!

Cartaz Caribbean Cuisine

Cartaz Fine Caribbean Cusine is located at 233 Ralph Avenue in Bedford Stuyvesant which is essentially in the Brooklyn borough. From the experience with this restaurant, you can find yourself getting great serivce and great food. They have a multitude of variety when it comes to Caribbean Cusine ranging from platters such as jerk chicken, oxtail, curry goat, bake chicken, brown stew chicken and so much more. Their main dishes usually come with other side dishes that include vegetables and plantains so you're really getting a good bang for your buck. The prices are fairly reasonable just because you are getting so much quantity of food that one order can serve you two meals. They also have an abundant pasta menu that includes larget buttered shrimp rasta pasta, chicken alfredo, shrimp alfredo, jerk chickn rasta pasta and others. They even make incredible roti ranging from curry shrimp, stew chicken, oxtail and curry goat roti. For their dessert menu, you can find yourself having some incredible chocolate cake or even just a classic white cake. The options here are endless! One of favorite dishes in Caribbean Cuisine are the jerk chicken and stew chicken because they present so many different rich flavors, bursting in your mouth. Cartaz Caribbean Cuisine is surely one place to go if you're looking for rich, authentic and flavorful Caribbean food.

Footprints Cafe

Footprints Cafe also located in Brooklyn has three differnet locations, one on Clarendon Road, the other on Surf Avenue and lastly another on Flatbush Avenue. This is another great place that has been serving amazing Caribbean food since 2001. They are known to to be home of the rasta pasta as this is definitely one of their signature dishes. Footprints Cafe makes customers have a choice when it comes to what they want to order with their rasta pasta. When Robert Bobby Gordon first opended the cafe, he was just an immigrant pioneering businessman who wanted to share the love he has for his culture. And so, he brought with him great-tasting, authentic and popular Jamaican dishes like jerk chicken, curry goat and oxtails to his restaurant. This restaurant truly has premier Caribbean elements complemented by modern international influences. They have also have featured in local newspapers such as New York Carib News, Caribbean Life News, Caribbean Times News and even local new outlets like Brooklyn News 12. There is such a variety when it comes to Footprints Cafe because they incorporate so many infamous Caribbean dishes such as seafood curry platters, seafood pocket platters, steam cabbage, all different types of roti and obviously as everyone knows them for, their rasta pasta paired with any meat you'd like. The food here is actually so impeccable and the establishment does a great job making sure all their customers are catered for and helped. They even have occasional special events happening at the restaurant ranging from karaoke nights to being a part of New York City restaurant week which is a semiannual program celebrating New York City's world-famous dining scenes. Restaurants particapting in this will offer special deals for some of their infamous dishes or even new ones so that customers can get a taste of great food but for a more affordable price. Overall, Footprints Cafe is surely one spot you do not want to miss out when craving Caribbean food or if you're in the mood to try something new!
